Who am I:

I am a third year Computer Engineering major at Simon Fraser University. My largest interests are game development, hiking, and reading. My most significant passion is for connecting the complexity of software, with the boundless art of mechanical design.

Overtime, my projects have progressed from building cardboard forts, creating massive popsicle stick boats, and making lightweight gliders. In the present, I work on multiplayer game development, mechanical design for robots to play soccer, and learning my classes through action and experience instead of memorization.

Major Projects:

Robot Soccer

Mechanics sub-team of the SFU club for Robot Soccer. Our goal is to compete in the 2024 robocup small size league in the Netherlands.

Evolution

A simulation about evolution via natural selection. The creatures brains and bodies evolve as they compete for limited resources. WASD for camera movement, scroll wheel to zoom, and left click for selection (to open the info panel for creatures).

Break it Down

In the arcade-like puzzle and finesse game, Break it Down, your goal is to deconstruct objects with your AI partner. Choose the right tool for the job, but be quick; you will get less time per deconstruction as the game progresses.
